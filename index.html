01

Two years at Duke, told in fragments

How an accidental all-nighter turned a marketing degree into a habit.

Master of Engineering Management, class of 2026. Came in from a job that was technically project management and practically translation. Three iOS products, 65 million users, one bilingual seat in the middle turning engineering specs into something a Japanese enterprise client would actually buy. The work was marketing in everything but title. The MEM was the part where I admitted it.

The pivot to the title happened in the worst possible way. A marketing group project, day before the presentation, and the deliverable suddenly became one person’s job. Pulled an all-nighter, gave a presentation that was passable at best, and somewhere around 4 a.m. figured out that the case study was actually interesting. That was the moment marketing stopped being a foreign language. Took over the Duke MEM social media account a few months later and watched the numbers move past anything the account had done before.
